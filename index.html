About me

Analytical thinking. Practical execution.

I am Dani Omar El-Rajab, an engineering and management-focused graduate from Germany. I completed my Bachelor's degree in Real Estate Engineering and Management at the University of Stuttgart in March 2026 and have continued my academic path with a Master's degree in Industrial Engineering and Management at KIT, which I began in April 2026.

My interests lie at the intersection of engineering, infrastructure, business, digitalization and technology. I am especially interested in how modern tools such as artificial intelligence, computer vision, automation, robotics and digital workflows can improve the way construction and infrastructure projects are planned, managed, inspected and delivered.

Alongside my studies, I gained practical experience in Project Control and Technical Project Management at Projekt Stuttgart-Ulm GmbH, working on the Stuttgart 21 airport connection. This role gave me valuable insight into large-scale infrastructure development, technical documentation, commissioning processes, workflow optimization and communication with different project stakeholders.

I enjoy understanding complex systems, structuring information clearly, improving processes — and developing ideas that can become real solutions.

My long-term goal is to connect engineering knowledge, management skills and entrepreneurial thinking to build useful, scalable and future-oriented projects.

My story

Shaped in Stuttgart. Built for what's next.

My journey started in Stuttgart, Germany, where I was born. Growing up in the Stuttgart region shaped my connection to engineering, infrastructure, mobility and business from an early age. After kindergarten, I attended elementary school at Steinbachschule in Büsnau, Stuttgart, continued at Hegel-Gymnasium Vaihingen, and completed my Abitur at Gymnasium Unterrieden in 2020.

During my school years, I developed an early interest in business and independence. At the age of 16, I started my first small resale project, earning my first few hundred euros. Although it was a small beginning, it gave me real experience with demand, pricing, communication, risk, responsibility and profit — and showed me that creating value and taking initiative leads to real results.

After my Abitur, I first studied Business Administration at Stuttgart University of Applied Sciences, building a foundation in economics and management. I then moved into a more technical and interdisciplinary direction: Real Estate Engineering and Management at the University of Stuttgart — a programme connecting civil engineering, real estate economics, project planning, property development, business administration and technical management. I graduated in March 2026.

During my Bachelor's studies, I became increasingly fascinated by digitalization and automation in construction. My academic work focused on the use of artificial intelligence for drone-based inspections and image analysis — exploring how deep learning models such as VGG16, ResNet and EfficientNet can detect cracks in concrete structures and support automated inspection processes.

Today, I continue this path through my Master's degree in Industrial Engineering and Management at KIT — deepening my understanding of the connection between engineering, management, technology, economics and innovation. My goal: managing complex technical systems, building scalable solutions, and contributing to the digital transformation of engineering and infrastructure.
